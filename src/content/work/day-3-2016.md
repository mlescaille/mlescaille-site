A little more over 3 days, right?

I did said that the committment depended on my workload.

Anyway, day 3 of the 66 days of challenge was exciting, I got to code a reverse array algorithm and crafted some of the problems of Programming Pearls. Also looked at other ways to implement my already implemented version of BinarySearch in Java.

In the process I discovered, *yes, that means I didn’t know*, that the Java API doesn’t have a reverse algorithm in its Arrays interface, is worth noting that I was exclusively working with primitives and the Collections reverse didn’t serve my purpose. It’s fun how working with high level APIs, systems and designs one might forget the joy that this little algorithm implementations brings me. In my day to day job I rarely get to implement algorithms and it’s most API design and consumption that anything else. Looking forward to finish these 66 days to find out how these two world maps in my mind.

Programming pearls is taking me more than expected. Obviously I misjudge the time it will take me to complete the book. I didn’t take into account the time one takes researching more of what the book offers and the algorithms. I think this is one of the perks of this journey.

That’s all for today folks!

Happy thinking and coding
