Sometimes I wonder—

how could they do it?

Take her,

leave her shoes behind.

Isn't the soul sewn into what we wear?

They said to not

ask why.

Don't judge.

What is in the other is in me.

They don't know

how this heart came to be.

I was her.

I will be her, any day now.

Still,

they ask for hands to hold,

they clean the room,

they carry on.

So we keep our stories flowing.

Invent new ones,

cover the paths behind us,

pray they never return.

They say:

you're only here to hold yourself.

Yet, I was passed

unspoken pain.

And she got hers.

My back aches

with wounds I've never met.

My body speaks

a language

taught long before

I had pain of my own to pass.

You see why I look up

and keep wondering?

Why up?

These lines have been

threaded from the ground.
